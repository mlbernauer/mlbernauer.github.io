Goals and Objectives

  1. Asess patient-specific disease states in hospitalized patients

    • Retieve patient data pertinent to disease states
    • Interpret and analyze important supplemental information including laboratory data and diagnostic studies.
    • Determine status, etiology, and risk factors for thrombosis or bleeding.
    • Synthesize and prioritize problems from patient history, examination, and laboratory data.

  2. Evaluate patient-specific anticoagulant drug therapy and therapeutic problems

    • Assess need for and appropriateness of anticoagulant therapy
    • Assess compliance
    • Identify and assess actual and potential adverse drug reactions
    • Report any actual ADRs
    • Identify, assess and communicate actual and potential drug interactions

  3. Establish a collaborative environment with healthcare team(s) and patient

    • Interact professionally with all health care professionals
    • Interact professional with patients and patient families
    • Apply clinical skills in a multidisciplinary, patient care setting
    • Document pharmaceutical care activities
    • Educate healthcare providers, patients, and peerson drug-related information

  4. Establish and demonstrate professional communication skills

    • Communicate in a facilitative, effective, and efficient manner with other members of the healthcare team, other healthcare providers, the patient and the patients family
    • Provdie patient and drug related information and recommendations to other healthcare professionals after consultation with the preceptor
    • Formulate and defend conclusions/rationale regarding drug thearpy
    • Write concise, legible, complete and organized chart notes

  5. Select and recommend a comprehensive anticogulant drug therapy plan

    • Recommend appropriate drug thearapy and explain rational for thearpy
    • Develop theraputic goals
    • Recommend non-pharmacologic thearpy
    • Revise drug therapy plan to achieve desired outcomes
    • Evaluate and assist in managing specific adverse drug reactions and/or drug interactions
    • Monitor drug thearpy by identifying and following relavant parameters at appropriate intervals/frequencies

  6. Display professional behavior

    • Provide patient-centered care
    • Practice ethically
    • Demonstrate responsibility
    • Maintain patient confidentiality
    • Display indepependent self-learning
    • Comply with all laws and regulations governing the practice of pharmacy

Course requirements

Clinical

  1. Participate in anticoagulation consults requested by various medical services
  2. Participate in daily review of anticoagulation thearpy reports and provide drug information and clinical expertise as related to anticoagulated patients.
  3. Be responsible for drug monitoring, obtaining medication histories, providing drug therapy consults to physicians and patients.
  4. Present patients to preceptor daily.
  5. Complete follow up topics assigned by preceptor or consulting team.
  6. Students will be orally challenged throughout the rotation on applied drug knowledge by the preceptors and consulting services.

Student projects

Students may be responsible for up to two presentations. These may take the form of in-services, journal clubs or case presentations.

Education

Preceptor will make readings available to students. These are required and will be discussed.

Core content:

Core content will be covered by acutal patient care experiences, discussion of reading materials or topics, and/or patient presentations will include but not limited to the following:

  • Essential warfarin knowledge
  • Parenteral anticoagulants
  • VTE prophylaxis
  • New anticoagulants
  • Heparin-induced thrombocytopenia

Weekly activities

  1. The student will meet to discuss patients with preceptor every day
  2. At least one day a week will be set aside to discuss disease states and pharmacothearpy

Responsibilites

  1. Patient presentations: should include the following
    • Date of admission
    • Reason for admission (1 sentence)
    • Patient location
    • Past medical history (PMH)
    • Allergy history
    • Current medications (include start/stop dates)
    • Patients current status
    • Vitals (including height, weight)
    • Pertinent labs (including culture results and dates)
    • Teams plan for the patient
    • Limit presentation to a maximum of 10 minutes per patient
  2. Student is responsible for all dosing adjustments. Recommendations/interventions should be discussed with preceptor.
  3. Journal club: article no more than 6 months old. Presentations should be no longer than 30 mintues. Handout should be no longer than 2 pages. Handout should include a summary of the article with critique and appropriate references.
  4. Learning issues will be assigned for presentation at preceptor meetings
  5. Students will be expected to participate in disease state discussions. Will be expected to find an review pertinent medical articles as part of the discussion.
  6. Preceptor must be notified of anyalteration from assignment schedule.
