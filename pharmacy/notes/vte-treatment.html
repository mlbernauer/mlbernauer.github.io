VTE Treatment

Acute DVT/PE general management

General 2 drug therapy

  • Rapid anticoagulation with LMWH, IV UFH, SQ UFH with warfarin is necessary
    • LMWH or fondaparinux preferred over IV UFH or SQ UFH
      • Once daily LMWH is preferred to twice daily LMWH
      • Use caution in old age, poor renal function, post-surgical patients as once daily dosing causes anticoagulation spike and may increase risk of bleeding
      • Consider BID dosing in patients with high clot burden (saddle embolism)
    • Overlap parenteral anticoag with warfarin for a minimum of 5 days and until INR is 2-3 for at least 24 hours.
      • If INR > 3 prior to day 5; consider decreasing warfarin dose unless and continuing parenteral unless there is risk of bleed.
      • If INR > 3 prior to day 5 and there is bleed; consider discontinuing parenteral agent and continuing only warfarin
        • The reason for discontinuation of parenteral agent must be documented!

Direct Oral Anticoagulant (DOAC) Single Drug Therapy

  • Rivaroxaban and apixaban are both FDA approved for:
    • Treatment of DVT/PE
    • Reducing risk of recurrent DVT/PE after initial anticoagulant therapy.
  • Can be used without initial parenteral lead-in period.
  • Should not be overlapped with parenteral agent due to PK similarities
  • Inpatient anticoag services are required for use of rivaroxaban/apixaban for acute/recurrent VTE

Criteria for DOAC

  • Need based on acute VTE or recurrent VTE prevention
  • Patient able to afford drug; insurance, out of pocket, patient assistance
  • Creatinine clearance > 30 mL/min
  • Not on CYP3A4 P-
