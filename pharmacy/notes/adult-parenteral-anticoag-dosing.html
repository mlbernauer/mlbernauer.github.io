Adult parenteral anticoagulation dosing

Created 2016-01-10 by Michael L. Bernauer

VTE prophylaxis

High risk for clots

  • hip/knee surgery
  • trauma
  • spinal cord injury
  • Encourage use of enoxaparin over UFH in patients with CrCl > 30 mL/min and weight > 50 kg due to safety, efficacy, convenience and cost
Agent Risk for clot Usual dose CrCl < 30 ml/min BMI > 40 Wt < 50 kg Epidural/spinal anesthesia
Enoxaparin High (hip/knee surgery, spinal/trauma) 30mg SQ Q12 Relatively contraindicated, may use 30mg SQ QD 0.5mg/kg Q12 30 mg SC QD Contraindicated
Enoxaparin Moderate risk 40mg SQ QD Relatively contraindicated, may use 30 mg SQ QD or UFH 5000 SQ Q8h 0.5 mg/kg QD 30 mg SQ QD or UFH 5000 units SQ Q8H Allowed
UFH -- 5000 units SQ Q8H or 5000 units SQ Q12 if spinal/epidural No adjustment 7500 units SQ Q8H No adjustment, may consider 5000 U SQ Q12 in extreme cases Allowed
  • Measure Anti-Factor Xa enoxaparin + BMI > 40, Wt < 50kg, CrCl < 30 mL/min or pregnant
  • Do not use enoxaparin in overweight patients receiving spinal/epidural anesthesia unless receiving continuous peripheral nerve block.
  • Fondaparinux if Hx of HIT, pork allergy or religion

  • Enoxaparin preferred over UFH for acute VTE
  • Only use UFH if renal compromised or potential invasive procedure
  • High dose enoxaparin (1.5 mg/kg SQ QD) may result in spike in anticoagulant activity consider Q12 dosing in high bleed risk patients

Enoxaparin dosing

  • Large therapeutic window precludes monitoring
  • Can round to nearest syringe size (to nearest 10mg)
  • Available syringe sizes: 30mg, 40mg, 60mg, 80mg, 100mg, 120mg, 150mg
  • Consider rounding down if high bleed risk; consider rounding up in VTE/PT

UFH protocols

  • Dosing based on actual body weight
  • Bolus dose is recommended maximum starting dose but may be exceeded if necessary
  • Bolus shortens onset of action
  • Bolus is useful in patients who need rapid anticoagulation; may omit if high risk for bleed
Agent Indication Rationale Bolus Infusion Monitoring
UFH DVT/PE Rapid/assertive AC to prevent extension of thrombus and embolism 80 U/kg 18 U/kg/h Hep-Xa
UFH ACS/Stroke Less aggressive AC d/t antiplatelet therapy, post-thrombotic syndrome and hemorrhagic conversion 60 U/kg 12 U/kg/h Hep-Xa
UFH CT surgery, vascular surgery Prevent catheter-induced thromboembolism and thromboembolism during arterial and cardiac surgery No bolus 500-1000 U/hr aPTT (55--85s)
  • Restrict Fondaparinux for treatment of patients with
    • Suspected HIT
    • History of HIT
    • Pork allergy or religion
    • Use in patients > 165kg that can afford it to facilitate single injection therapy

Thrombolytic therapy

PE with hypotension (SBP < 90 mmHg)

  • rt-PA 100 mg over 2 hours is most common/studied dose
  • Bolus is indicated in current or imminent cardiac arrest
  • Administer through peripheral vein vs pulmonary artery catheter
  • Short infusion (2h) preferred over longer infusion (24h)
  • If possible; seek alternate intervention (CT surgery, vascular surgery, interventional radiology)

Acute DVT

  • rt-PA per IR or CT recommendation
  • Catheter directed thrombolysis (CDT) preferred over systemic thrombolysis
  • Dosing:
    • Infusion first technique
      • Unilateral catheter: 1mg/hr
      • Bilateral catheter: 0.5mg/hr
    • Monitor fibrinogen q6h
      • If fibrinogen < 150 reduce infusion by 50% and call attending
      • If fibrinogen < 100 stop infusion and call attending
      • Usual given with heparin 300-600 U/h (monitor aPTT)
    • Single session (Trellis procedure i.e. pharmacomechanical)
      • rt-PA 5-25mg/10mL
  • Patients that benefit from CDT
    • Ileofemoral DVT
    • Symptoms < 14 days
    • Good functional status
    • Life expectancy > 1 yr
    • Low bleed risk

Monitoring

Pharmacists may order necessary labs

  • Platelets (Heparin, enoxaparin): q3d to monitor for HIT
  • Serum creatinine (enoxaparin, fondaparinux): baseline prior to dispensing
    • May dispense single dose in emergency prior to SCr
  • Anti-Factor Xa:
    • In obesity (BMI > 40)
    • Underweight (ABW < 50kg)
    • Kidney disease (CrCl < 30 mL/min)
    • Pregnancy
  • Anti-Xa do not correlate with clinical outcomes; gross measure of over/under anticoagulation
