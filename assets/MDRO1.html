Describe Phenotypes¶

Here what I mean by phenotype is any particular pattern of resistance. I represent phenotypes as a pattern of 0's and 1's where 0's indicate sensitivity to that anibiotic and 1's represent resistance to that antibiotic. We tested 18 antibiotics so there are a total of $2^{18}=262,144$ possible phenotypes. However, we only find 201 unique phenotypes in our dataset. For example one of the Acinetobacters has a phenotype of 100111000001000000. The order of antibiotics is Ampicillin, Ampicillin.Sulbactam, Piperacillin.Tazobactam, Aztreonam, Cefazolin, Cefoxitin, Ceftriaxone, Ceftazidime, Cefepime, Imipenem, Meropenem, Ertapenem, Ciprofloxacin, Levofloxacin, Amikacin, Gentamicin, Tobramycin, Trimethoprim.Sulfamethoxazole.

# computing kappa matrix
kappa_matrix <- function(X){
    n <- ncol(X)
    mat <- matrix(NA, n, n)
    for(i in 1:n){
        for(j in 1:n){
            mat[i,j] <- cohen.kappa(table(X[,i], X[,j]))$kappa
        }
    }
    rownames(mat) <- colnames(X)
    colnames(mat) <- colnames(X)
    mat
}

Comparing Definitions according to Antibiotic Resistance Score¶

Enterobacteriaceae/Acinetobacter¶

Here I calcualte the antibiotic resistance score for Enterobacteriaceae and Acinetobacter while excluding Pseudomoanas. This is so that Pseudomonas does not influence the weighting of each antibiotics. The antibotic resistance score is caluclated by the following $$R = \sum_{i=1}^{i=n} x_{i}w_{i}$$ where $x_{i}$ is 1 if organism is resistant to antibiotic $i$ and $w_{i}$ is the weight for antibiotic $i$. Antibiotics are weighted so that "stronger" antibiotics recieve a larger weight and "weaker" antibiotics recieve a lower weight. This strength of each antibiotic is calculated by $$w_i = 1-Q_i$$ where $Q_i$ is the fraction of organisms resistant to antibiotic $i$. Once resistance scores are calculated, we can look at each MDRO definition and plot the resistance score distribution among those organisms the definitions classifies as MDRO and those that it classifies as non-MDRO. This is shown below in the box-plots where the resistance score for is plotted on the y-axis and the different definitions are plotted on the x-axis. Also, there are two plots, one for Enterobacter/Acinetobacter and one for Pseudomonas

ea <- subset(mdro, X != "Pseudomonas aeruginosa")
