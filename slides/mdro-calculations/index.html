Antibiotic Weights

Calculation

Each antibiotic \(i\) is given as weight \(w_i\), according to the number of organisms that are susceptible to that antibiotic. Antibiotics that have higher proportions of susceptible organisms are given higher weight. Weights can take on values \(0 \le w_i \le 1\)

\[w_i = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{j=1}^{n} S_{ij}\]

Where \(n\) is the total number of species included in the scoring definition and \(S_{ij}\) is the proportion of organisms in species \(j\) that are susceptible to antibiotic \(i\) and calculated as follows

\[S_{ij} = 1 - \frac{R_{ij}}{N_{j}}\]

\(R_{ij}\) is the number of organisms in species \(j\) that are resistant to antibiotic \(i\) and \(N_j\) is the total number of organism of species \(j\) (both susceptible and resistant).

Resistance Scores

Calculation

The restance score, \(Q_{k}\), is calculated for each organism \(k\) and is the weighted sum of antibiotics that the organism is resistant to and a can be expressed as

\[Q_{k} = w_{1}X_{1k} + w_{2}X_{2k} + ... + w_iX_{ik}\]

Where and \(X_i \in \{0,1\}\) depending on whether the organisms is susceptible or resistant to antibiotic \(i\). This can be show mor compactly as

\[Q_k = \sum_{i=1}^{n}w_iX_{ik}\]

Where \(n\) is the total number of antibiotics in the data set and \(k\) is the total number of organisms in the data set.
